<p>This Van Gogh letter site is a really nice example of the use of TEI and images to produce a dynamic and interesting site.</p><p>&nbsp;</p><p>Some of you may be interested in the new digital edition of all extant<br />letters from and to Vincent van Gogh we launched yesterday, available free<br />of charge&nbsp; at <a href="http://www.vangoghletters.org">http://www.vangoghletters.org</a>. As you can read in ‘About this<br />edition’&nbsp; (<a href="http://www.vangoghletters.org/vg/about_6.html">http://www.vangoghletters.org/vg/about_6.html</a>) the letters are<br />encoded in TEI. <br /><br />The edition is based on fifteen years of research. For each of the 902<br />letters the site gives a transcription of the original Dutch or French, a<br />translation into English, a full (zoomable)&nbsp; facsimile, comprehensive<br />annotation, and illustrations (about 2000 in all) of the works of art<br />discussed in the letters and annotations. <br /><br />We hope you enjoy it. <br /><br />Peter Boot</p>
