<p><strong>Average Velocity:</strong> 38 sp</p><p><strong>Velocity This Iteration:</strong> 40 sp</p><p><strong>Scott's Notes:</strong>&nbsp; Overall our last iteration went pretty well.&nbsp; We're getting more consistant in how we report problems and progress.&nbsp; We still need to communicate more effectively external to the deparatment but overall it's getting better.&nbsp; Strategists are doing better with planning as well. We're starting to have some more release planning being done.&nbsp; The downside is that it can raise anxieties coming to terms with where our deadlines are, but knowing you're headed for a wall is at least a step forward for us.</p><p>Our process needs better refinement as well and we need to settle on a metric that communicates progress better without getting in the way of development.&nbsp; Case in point is the use of Estimated capacity.&nbsp; In the past I've been reporting an estimated capactiy that was derived from a complete guess of 1sp per developer per day.&nbsp; That was a completely made up number and now that we have several iterations behind us I've moved to tracking our Average Velocity instead of estimated story points as a better reflection of what we're actually doing on an iteration by iteration basis.&nbsp; </p><p>Also our actual use of story points isn't very effective as we're using them as a replacement for "ideal days" instead of a measure of complexity of a task.&nbsp; This is generally because of the bootstrapping process I used, relating a point per day to develper and if I had it to do again I'm not sure I'd do it that way.&nbsp; That said, I'm not sure what else I would have done to get it all rolling.&nbsp; Our goal with Story Points however is to use those as a number relative to other tasks, not to a day.&nbsp; We should be taking some well known tasks and relating new tasks to how more or less complex they are relative to those tasks.&nbsp; This would allow us to "chunk" work much better and talk about what we're going to accomplish instead of how much work we're going to do.</p><p>In the weeks ahead we'll be moving more toward refinement of Story Points so expect that come out soon.</p><p><strong>Project - StoryPoints</strong></p><ul><li>Other - 3</li><li>PID Manager - 5.5</li><li>Reserves Direct - 4.5</li><li>Rushdie - 9.5</li><li>StaffWeb - 3</li><li>Slave Voyages - 10</li><li>DigWF - 2</li><li>dWrangler (prototype) - 2.5</li></ul>
