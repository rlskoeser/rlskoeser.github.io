<p><img src="/sites/techknowhow.library.emory.edu/files/images/thumb_foaf_graph_subset.png" width="90" height="90" style="margin: 5px; float: left;" />A few weeks ago, I spent a little bit of time on a Friday afternoon playing with some MARBL Finding Aids and trying to generating some FOAF RDF.&nbsp; I chose the four Irish Literature finding aids that I identified as having an Index of Selected Correspondents (<em>very</em> few of the MARBL finding aids have them, I imagine because it is very time-consuming to create).&nbsp; Other Finding Aids have names of correspondents in the container list, but this was an easier starting point (and even here, there was quite a bit of messiness in the names-- dates, noms de plume, titles, all capitalized or mixed case, etc).</p><p>I finally took the time to adapt a simple python script Ben had written, and converted the four FOAF xml files I'd generated into a GraphViz chart.&nbsp; The image below is the section of the chart I thought would be the most interesting to share (the entire thing is quite large), because you can see many of the common correspondents shared even just among these four people.</p><p>&nbsp;</p><p><img src="/sites/techknowhow.library.emory.edu/files/images/foaf_graph_subset.png" width="696" height="600" /></p>
