<p><strong>Esimated Capacity:</strong> 47 sp</p><p><strong>Planned User Stories:</strong> 27 sp<br /><strong>Actual Completed:</strong>&nbsp; 37 sp ! (What the heck?)</p><p><strong>Scott's Comments:</strong>&nbsp; We had some significant problems that affected our productivity and reporting this iteration and I'll talk about those major factors below.</p><ol><li><strong>Poor Iteration Re-estimation:</strong>&nbsp; We had a number of tasks take longer than originally anticipated and developers did not re-estimate the story points for a task once it became apparent these would take longer.&nbsp; In the future we will refer to our current tasks in the stand-up meeting in terms of story points done and remaining and re-estimate during the stand-up as soon as we're aware the story points for a task have changed.&nbsp; This will help everyone identify problem areas earlier as well as continue to have a discussion about the relative priority of a task as it grows beyond original estimates.&nbsp; The result of this is that we may have actually accomplished more Story Points than we're listing but wouldn't know right now because we failed to re-estimate properly.</li><li><strong>Lack of Initiative in Picking up new tasks:</strong>&nbsp; Overall developers weren't proactive about picking up new cards or viewing what was on the board as a particular priority.&nbsp; We discussed this at our iteration debrief and everyone was clear that what is on the board is a priority and tasks not on the board need to be added and tracked if they are suppose to be a priority.</li></ol><p>Overall, preformance on this iteration was poor.&nbsp; We didn't track what we needed to track or communicate expanding work in an effecive way.&nbsp; Part of the reason for this continues to be poor overall planning and prioritization across the library.&nbsp; As tasks change and expand it becomes difficult to stay focused as developers usually have to go back and reasses priorities themselves and the overall impression in this iteration was that we had a poor grasp of where we should be going specifically with many projects. </p><p>The last statement I'd make about the iteration is that I'm encouraged by the discovery of these problems and take it as a sign that this process is working for us.&nbsp; In the past we would have many of these difficulties and continue on with them for months without identifying or addressing them.&nbsp; With this process we are able to address them after two weeks and take action to refocus and adjust the way we're workign to change them.&nbsp; Additionally I think each of the developers seemed to come out of this iteration with a more concrete understanding of how to improve performance overall and how specific actions relate to productivity and accomplishing goals.</p><p><strong>Project - SP (esitmated/completed)</strong></p><ul><li>Rushdie Project - 0/5</li><li>Reserves Direct - 3.5/5</li><li>Drupal Sites - 2/2</li><li>Slave Voyages - 5/9</li><li>StaffWeb - 2.5/4</li><li>Repositories - 3.5/4</li><li>DigWF - 6/4</li><li>Digital Programs Site - 3.5/0.5</li><li>PID Manager - 2.5/0</li><li>Other 3/3.5</li></ul>
